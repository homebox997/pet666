Heart Conditions Are More Common Than You Think

Heart Disease in Senior Dogs: Complete Guide & Case Study

Heart disease affects up to 10% of all dogs — and 75% of senior dogs develop some form of heart murmur. Early diagnosis and treatment can extend and improve your dog's life.

Common Types of Heart Disease in Dogs

Degenerative Valve Disease (MVD) — Most common in small-to-medium breeds. The heart valves wear out and leak blood backward. Common in Cavalier King Charles, Poodles, and Chihuahuas.

Dilated Cardiomyopathy (DCM) — More common in large breeds (Dobermans, Boxers, Great Danes). The heart muscle becomes weak and cannot pump effectively.

Heart Murmurs — An abnormal sound heard during a heartbeat cycle. Not all murmurs are serious, but they should always be evaluated by a vet.

Warning Signs of Heart Disease

Persistent coughing, especially worse at night or after exercise
Labored or rapid breathing, even at rest
Reduced stamina and tiring quickly on walks
Fainting or collapsing episodes
Swollen belly (fluid buildup from heart failure)
Pale or blue-tinged gums
Sudden weight loss and muscle wasting
Restlessness, especially at night

Heart Disease Care & Treatment

Regular Vet Checkups & Scans

Annual heart checks (stethoscope exam, X-rays, blood tests, and in some cases ECG or ultrasound) are essential for monitoring progression.

Heart Medication

ACE inhibitors, diuretics, and positive inotropes are commonly prescribed. Never adjust or stop medication without veterinary consultation.

Controlled Exercise

Short, gentle walks are better than long or strenuous exercise. Watch for signs of fatigue, heavy panting, or rapid breathing and rest accordingly.

Low-Sodium Diet

Reducing salt intake helps minimize fluid retention. Prescription cardiac diets (Hill's h/d, Royal Canin Cardiac) are formulated for heart patients.

Keep Calm & Comfortable

Stress worsens heart symptoms. Maintain a calm environment, avoid hot weather, and keep your dog at a comfortable, cool temperature.
