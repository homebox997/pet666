Arthritis in Senior Dogs

Arthritis affects 1 in 4 senior dogs in Australia. Early detection and proper management can dramatically improve your dog's quality of life.

📖 What Is Canine Arthritis?

Arthritis (osteoarthritis or degenerative joint disease) is a progressive condition where the protective cartilage in joints wears down over time, causing pain, stiffness, and reduced mobility.

It most commonly affects the hips, elbows, knees, and spine — and is especially prevalent in dogs over 7 years old. Large breeds and overweight dogs are at higher risk.

⚠️ Common Symptoms

🔹Stiffness after rest, especially in the morning
🔹Difficulty getting up from lying down
🔹Limping or favoring one or more legs
🔹Reluctance to jump, climb stairs, or run
🔹Slowing down on walks or shorter walks
🔹Licking or chewing at joints
🔹Muscle loss in affected limbs
🔹Irritability when touched in certain areas

🩺 Severity Levels

Mild: Slight stiffness, occasional limp — responds well to supplements and weight management
Moderate: Noticeable stiffness, reduced activity — requires supplements + pain management
Severe: Difficulty moving, significant pain — needs vet-prescribed medication and lifestyle changes

💡 Care Tips

🏋️ Maintain a Healthy Weight

Excess weight puts enormous stress on joints. Even a 5% reduction in body weight can significantly reduce pain in arthritic dogs.

🦴 Joint Supplements

Glucosamine, chondroitin, MSM, and omega-3 fatty acids (fish oil) can help reduce inflammation and support cartilage health.

🛋️ Comfortable Sleeping Surface

Orthopedic dog beds with memory foam cushion joints and reduce pressure points. Keep bedding warm — cold worsens stiffness.

🚶 Gentle, Regular Exercise

Short daily walks (15–30 min) on soft surfaces help maintain muscle and joint flexibility. Avoid running on hard ground.

🏠 Home Modifications

Ramps for cars and furniture, non-slip rugs on slippery floors, and raised food bowls can make daily life much easier.
