Kidney Disease in Senior Dogs

Chronic kidney disease (CKD) affects 1 in 3 senior dogs. It progresses slowly — early detection through regular vet checks can make a huge difference.

📖 What Is Kidney Disease?

Chronic kidney disease (CKD) means the kidneys gradually lose their ability to filter waste from the blood, regulate hydration, and maintain electrolyte balance.

It is irreversible but manageable — with the right diet, medication, and monitoring, many dogs with CKD live comfortably for months to years after diagnosis.

⚠️ Warning Signs

🔹Drinking significantly more water than usual
🔹Frequent urination or accidents in the house
🔹Unexplained weight loss despite normal appetite
🔹Loss of appetite or selective eating
🔹Dull, dry coat and excessive shedding
🔹Lethargy and weakness
🔹Bad breath with a chemical/ammonia smell
🔹Vomiting or loose stools

💡 Care & Management

🥗 Kidney-Friendly Diet

Low-phosphorus, reduced-protein prescription diets (like Royal Canin Renal or Hill's k/d) reduce kidney workload. Always transition diet gradually under vet guidance.

💧 Keep Fresh Water Available

Dogs with kidney disease need constant access to clean water. Consider multiple water bowls, a pet fountain, or adding water to food to encourage hydration.

🩺 Regular Vet Monitoring

Blood tests every 3–6 months track kidney values (creatinine, BUN, SDMA). Early detection of worsening allows timely treatment adjustments.

💊 Supplements & Medication

Phosphate binders, omega-3 supplements, and in some cases, appetite stimulants or blood pressure medication may be prescribed by your vet.

🧘 Reduce Stress

Keep routines consistent, avoid strenuous exercise, and provide a quiet, comfortable resting space. Stress can worsen symptoms.
